Nido:
an editorial project

Nido | A magazine about nonconventional interiors.

Nido is an independent interior magazine in Italian language. It questions the intrinsic meaning and minimum requirements that define the concept of "home", “shelter”, proposing a selection of intertwined visual content with the reflections of numerous external collaborators.

This hypothesis of magazine was born from scratch. Start with an imaginary newsroom that offers an imaginary proposal, created in function of a university course (the real one, luckily).
Both the jury-rigged editorial staff (a.k.a. ourself) and the course exists only thanks to meetings digital on Microsoft Teams, which replaces the physicality of the work table and classrooms and that allows you to talk and see inside of special "rooms".

Also Teams is a small nest, in which, from etymology, you "is".
I think it’s just this idea who created the spark for the idea of Nido, which, despite the distance, we have reached in sync.

Forced into our homes, we started to think of interiors, ways of living to objects, then also to the houses of others who, like us, has been in quarantine.

How do all the others live? What are the minimum requirements to talk about "home"? Are not houses also the rooms in which you create your own space of personalization, the prison cells, the rooms of the sick, the tent cities, the houses Furniture, nomadic homes, temporary homes? By answering these questions, we have created Nido.
