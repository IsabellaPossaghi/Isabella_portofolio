Nido:
an editorial project

The Rotten Tube | An analysis of YT's content farms made for kids.

YouTube is one of the most used platforms to entertain kids, so popular that it overcame television* and this does not necessarily happen under supervision.
Parents and babysitters give their children access to it, trusting its system and educational purposes. Left alone in front of the screen, children go through a great amount of videos, consuming anything recommended, as decided by YouTube’s algorithm.

This hypothesis of magazine was born from scratch. Start with an imaginary newsroom that offers an imaginary proposal, created in function of a university course (the real one, luckily). Both the jury-rigged editorial staff (a.k.a. ourself) and the course exists only thanks to meetings digital on Microsoft Teams, which replaces the physicality of the work table and classrooms and that allows you to talk and see inside of special "rooms".

Also Teams is a small nest, in which, from etymology, you "is". I think it’s just this idea who created the spark for the idea of Nido, which, despite the distance, we have reached in sync.

Forced into our homes, we started to think of interiors, ways of living to objects, then also to the houses of others who, like us, has been in quarantine.

How do all the others live? What are the minimum requirements to talk about "home"? Are not houses also the rooms in which you create your own space of personalization, the prison cells, the rooms of the sick, the tent cities, the houses Furniture, nomadic homes, temporary homes? By answering these questions, we have created Nido.
